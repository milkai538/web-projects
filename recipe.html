A Quick Hummus Recipe

Hummus is a thick paste commonly paired with salads, grilled meats, pita breads, and other Greek and Middle Eastern dishes. This recipe I learned from numerous different recipes over the years will teach you how to quickly make some of the tasty hummus with as little mess as possible.


What You'll Need

  • One can (400g) of chick peas (garbanzo beans)
  • 175g of tahini
  • Six sundried tomatoes
  • Half of a red pepper
  • A pinch of cayenne pepper
  • One clove of garlic
  • A dash of olive oil

What You're Here For

  1. Start by removing the skin from the garlic, and chop it coarsely
  2. Remove all of the seeds and stalk from the pepper, and chop coarsely
  3. Add all of the ingredients into a food processor
  4. Process all of the ingredients into a paste
  5. If you want a coarse, and "chunky" hummus, process it for a short time
  6. If you want a smooth hummus, process it for a longer time.

If you'd prefer different flavors, try blending in a small measure of lemon and coriander, chili pepper, lime and chipotle, harissa and mint, or spinach and feta cheese. Experiment and see what works best for you!! Don't forget to store it!!


How To Store It

Once you finish using your tasty hummus, seal the leftovers in a good container! You should be able to use it for just around a week after you've made it, but throw it out if it starts getting all fizzy. Hummus can be frozen, but you gotta remember to thaw it and use it within a couple of months!
