back to sci-fi cause I read more than just one premise in this genre

Tokyo Ghoul is a sci-fi horror series that I would never recommend watching over reading because, like most, its show is not nearly good enough for the books it was. Back to what it's about though; Ghouls are in-fact a worldwide species of extremely human-like human hunters evolved to eat humans, but only focuses on the ones in Tokyo(Obviously). Some of the things I love most about the series is its in-depth descriptions of how Ghoul biology works, political themes, action, and how ghouls and their eating can be read in different ways. (skip to highlighted text to avoid rant) The main differences they have from humans is an abundance of RC (Red Child, named for their appearance to a fetus and obvious coloration) cells (humans may have a couple hundred at most but ghouls have between 1000-8000 normally.), the organ and retractable limb(s) meant for hunting humans, eye color changes, and inability to consume anything that isn't a human. Throughout the series' 2 parts we watch the main character, Ken Kaneki, a human turned half-ghoul, travel through numerous different perspectives, and inevitably reach a conclusion to how he thinks the world of humans and ghouls should live, along with his perspective on the world in general. I think (and therefore I am NOTE: I can totally be wrong here as I've only read the series twice.) the ghouls and their eating of people can be read in a couple different ways, especially for different characters. Maybe it's only supposed to be one thing; Take what I hypothesize with a grain of salt, or a pound think for yourself. I've noticed how ghouls can be read as transgender, as Kaneki himself states (in the beginning) that he is "A human in the body of a ghoul", and a different half-ghoul is outright transgender, but didn't use the term. For some ghouls, eating can be read as something intimate, something to be ashamed of, partaking in a mixture of wrath, gluttony, and maybe a tint of lust maybe a few, and/or just plain surviving. It's not like every ghoul is a violent and bloodthirsty (literally) monster, as the first group of ghouls we meet is a group that scavenges humans out of a lack of desire to murder and not weakness, but not to imply some of them aren't plain weak as the group feeds weak and scared ghouls too. The violent ones are easy to notice, for example: the first ghoul Kaneki encounters is colloquially known as "The Binge Eater" due to her choosing to eat far more than the bare minimum requirement of one per month.
