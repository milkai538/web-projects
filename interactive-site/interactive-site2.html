The Ichabod Campaign:


Ichabod, you're probably thinking of Ichabod Crane, but I'm sorry to tell you that this is nowhere near as nice a tale. The Ichabod Campaign is a GOC initiative that began in the 1970's and has always faced extreme negativity. The goal of the initiative is to get rid of reality benders as much as possible and as quickly as possible due to the cost their existence forces us to pay. It is not Mary Sue who pays for living her dream, but Joe Shmoe and you who are erased for whatever happy-go-lucky or edgelord desire a type green has. Repeat this as often as you can: Humanity outweighs one. The Cornwall Incident was the result of not only the Ichabod Campaign, but the backlash. Infamous ex-member of the GOC, Agent Ukulele, had hid a reality bending, romantic partner of his for 10+ years despite being the most contributing member to the Campaign itself, and even being considered extremist in his anti-reality-bender views. This all came as an even bigger shock to the reveal of Agent Ukulele being a Type Green entity himself, although his choice to be a reality sink (Reality maintainer) was not as surprising. During the Cornwall Incident the previously mentioned Type Green had developed into a Type Black entity. As of now, it is unknown how many else are capable of this, but there have been XXX known cases, and this is all coincided with the fact that she knew of her powers since birth. Most Type Greens develop them a bit before the age of 10, and fortunately don't become aware of them at said age, but since birth she has had inhuman features which gave way to her realization. With this, we come to the highly brief explanation of how to get rid of a Mary Sue: They have human weaknesses. It's always about perspective. They make things too perfect, they don't think every change through, they have feelings, they're not immortal (usually), and the 2 most important pieces -- They are capable of being damaged, and have limited reaction time.

And at the end?

We're all wishful thinkers. You likely hate this idea almost as much as we do, but it exists. Sometimes, I like to lay back in bed, and imagine a world in which there isn't even the concept of a reality bender, but.. I always get a call during these times for another mission. It's at that point where I'm forced to remember. -- This is it. There is no end. Not now, not ever. No end, and no honor.
